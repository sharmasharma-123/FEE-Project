Aam Aadmi Party (AAP)

Born out of the anti-corruption movement led by Anna Hazare, AAP is a young party that swept Delhi politics. It focuses on education, healthcare, and affordable utilities for the common citizen.

Founded: 2012 Ideology: Populist / Centrist Symbol: Broom HQ: New Delhi

Brief History

AAP was founded on 26 November 2012 by Arvind Kejriwal and associates from the India Against Corruption movement. In 2013, it won 28 seats in Delhi and formed a minority government briefly. In 2015, it achieved a landslide — 67 out of 70 seats. The party also governs Punjab since 2022. AAP is known for its focus on governance delivery and welfare-based politics.

Ideology & Vision

AAP describes itself as people-centric and anti-corruption. It leans centrist with left-leaning welfare policies. The party emphasises free or subsidised electricity, water, public schools, and hospitals. It focuses heavily on governance transparency and participatory democracy (mohalla sabhas).
