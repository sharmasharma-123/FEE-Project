Indian National Congress (INC)

One of the oldest political parties in the world, INC led India's freedom movement and governed the country for most of its post-independence history. It stands for secularism, social justice, and inclusive growth.

Founded: 1885 Ideology: Centrist Symbol: Hand HQ: New Delhi

Brief History

INC was founded on 28 December 1885 by A.O. Hume, Dadabhai Naoroji, and Dinshaw Wacha. It led the Indian independence movement under Mahatma Gandhi, Jawaharlal Nehru, and others. After independence, Nehru became the first PM. The party has produced leaders like Indira Gandhi, Rajiv Gandhi, and Manmohan Singh.

Ideology & Vision

Congress follows secularism, social democracy, and liberalism. It champions minority rights, reservation policies, and welfare programs. The party believes in a pluralist India with space for all religions and communities. Economic approach is mixed — supports both public sector and market reforms.
